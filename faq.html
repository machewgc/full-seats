About Precinct Captains

What does a Precinct Captain do?

A Precinct Captain engages members of his or her community and provides them a voice in the party. Captains learn the concerns of their community, ensure their neighbors’ voices are heard, and deliver that message to the Democratic Party to help shape the party platform. Precinct Captains are a mouthpiece for voters within the Democratic Party.

Here are some repsonsibilities of a Precinct Captain.

  • Obtain a list of registered Democrats and Independents in your precinct
  • Contact voters to create a connection and community within your precinct
  • Provide voters with information on upcoming elections and the Democratic Party platform
  • Canvass or phonebank your precinct for candidates in upcoming elections
  • Provide information on the voting process including get-out-the-vote contacts and mail-in or absentee voting
  • Represent your precinct’s voters in County Democratic Party meetings
  • Collect petition signatures for progressive causes
  • Recruit passionate neighbors to participate!

How many Precinct Captains can be in a precinct?

The Democratic and Republican parties each have one Committeeman and one Committeewoman per precinct.

Are there other names for this position?

Precinct Captains go by many names, and you may hear someone refer to them by a variety of other names.

  • Precinct Committeewoman\Committeeman
  • Precinct Chairwoman\Chairman
  • Precinct Committee Officer
  • Precinct Captain
  • Precinct Delegate

How to apply

How do I apply?

On the maps page, you will find a download link for the Candidacy Form. You will need to fill out this form, have it notarized, and mail it to your county election office. Your name will then appear on the ballot for Precinct Committeeman/Committeewoman in the next primary election.

How do I get my form notarized?

There are many Notary Publics available in Kansas and you can search for them here. Here are some places you can often find notary services.

  • Your local bank
  • City and town clerks’ offices
  • Courts
  • Real estate, insurance or law offices
  • Travel Agencies
  • Your local drug store or pharmacy
  • FedEx Kinkos
  • UPS Store
You do not have to be a client of the bank or business to use a notary public. Notaries are public servants. Some notaries may charge a fee for service if you are not an existing client of theirs.

Is there an application fee?

No. There is no fee for subitting your declaration of candidacy for Precinct Captain.

Where do I send my declaration of Candidacy?

Your declaration of candidacy must be mailed to your county election office. On the map page you can search your address to find your county election office address.

Do I already have a Precinct Captain that I would be unseating?

Probably not. We are in a tough spot here in Kansas with only about one fifth of precincts having a Chairperson. We will be updating the map with existing precinct captain names once we have all of the data collected from Kansas counties. If you do have one, reach out to them and get a feel for what they are about. Competition is healthy and we want the best Precinct Captains we can have.

Do I need approval from my party?

No, but you must be a registered Democrat.

Precinct Captain is an elected position, and you are free to submit your declaration of candidacy to appear on the ballot. If you’d like to get involved before your next primary election, contact your local Democratic Party; empty Captain positions can be appointed by the County Chairperson.
